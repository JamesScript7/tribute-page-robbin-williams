Robin Williams

America's beloved Actor, Comedian, and Friend

A timeline of Robin McLaurin Williams' life:

  • 1951 - Born in Chicago, Illinois, U.S. on July 21st to Laurie McLaurin and Anselm J. McLaurin.
  • 1963 - (Age 12) His family moved to a 40-room farmhouse on 20 acres in Bloomfield Hills, Michigan.
  • 1967 - (Age 16) His father took early retirement and the family moved to Tiburon, California.
  • 1969 - (Age 18) At the time of his High School graduation, he was voted "Most Likely Not to Succeed" and "Funniest."
  • 1973 - (Age 22) Attains full scholarship to the Julliard School in New York City.
  • 1974 - (Age 23) Worked as a mime in Central Park and performed stand-up comedy.
  • 1977 - (Age 26) Moved to LA and continued doing stand-up shows, including Comedy Club, when TV producer George Schlatter asked him to appear in the Laugh-In show. It becomes his debut TV appearance.
  • 1978 - 1982 (Age 27-31) Casted by Gary Marshall as the alien Mork of the hit TV series Happy Days. He gained so much popularity that it led to a spin-off hit television sitcom, Mork and Mindy. It had a weekly audience of 60 million and was credited with turning Williams into a "superstar."
  • 1978 - (Age 27) Married his first wife, Valerie Velardi.
  • 1979 - (Age 28) Appeared on the cover of Time and Rolling Stone magazine.
  • 1980 - (Age 29) First Major performance was as the title character in Popeye.
  • 1982 - (Age 31) Starred as a leading character in The World According to Garp.
  • 1983 - (Age 32) Had a small role in The Survivors.
  • 1983 - (Age 32) Had his first son Zachary Pym "Zak" Williams
  • 1984 - (Age 33) Movie: Moscow on the Hudson.
  • 1986 - (Age 35) Had a small role in Club Paradise.
  • 1987 - (Age 36) Recieves his first major break in starring role in director Barry Levinson's Good Morning, Vietnam. It earned him a nomination for the Academy Award for Best Actor.
  • 1988 - (Age 37) Divorced his first wife, Velardi.
  • 1988 - (Age 37) Movie: The Adventures of Baron Munchausen.
  • 1989 - (Age 38) Married Marsha Garces, Zachary's nanny. Had two children, Zelda Rae Williams (1989) and Cody Alan Williams (1991).
  • 1989 - (Age 38) Movie: Dead Poets Society. Nominated for Best Actor.
  • 1990 - (Age 39) Movie: Awakenings
  • 1991 - (Age 40) Played an adult Peter Pan in the movie Hook. Had to lose 25 pounds for the role.
  • 1991 - (Age 40) Movie: The Ficher King. Nominated for Best Actor.
  • 1992 - (Age 41) Movie: Aladdin. Voice of the Genie was written specifially for Williams. Won him a Golden Globe.
  • 1992 - (Age 41) Movie: FernGully: The Last Rainforest. Voice role.
  • 1993 - (Age 42) Movie: Mrs. Doubtfire.
  • 1995 - (Age 44) Movie: Jumanji.
  • 1996 - (Age 45) Movie: The Birdcage.
  • 1996 - (Age 45) Movie: Jack.
  • 1997 - (Age 46) Movie: Good Will Hunting.
  • 1997 - (Age 46) Movie: Flubber.
  • 1998 - (Age 47) Movie: What Dreams May Come. Won the Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor for Good Will Hunting.
  • 1998 - (Age 47) Movie: Patch Adams
  • 1999 - (Age 48) Movie: Bicentennial Man.
  • 2001 - (Age 50) Movie: A.I. Artificial Intelligence. Voice role.
  • 2002 - (Age 51) Movie: Insomnia. Movie: One Hour Photo.
  • 2003 - (Age 52) Checked himself into a substance-abuse rehabilitation center.
  • 2005 - (Age 54) Movie: Robots. Voice role.
  • 2006 - (Age 55) Movie: Happy Feet. Voice role.
  • 2006 - (Age 55) Movie: The Night Listener.
  • 2006 - (Age 55) Movie: Everyone's Hero.
  • 2009 - (Age 58) Hospitalized due to heart problems.
  • 2009 - (Age 58) Movie: World's Greatest Dad.
  • 2010 - (Age 59) Divorced his second wife, Garces.
  • 2011 - (Age 60) Married his third wife, Susan Schneider.
  • 2011 - (Age 60) Movie: Happy Feet Two. Voice role.
  • 2014 - (Age 63) Admitted himself to an Addiction Treatment Center for treatment related to Alcoholism.
  • 2014 - (Age 63) Death. Suicide by hanging.

Throughout his career he recieved two Primetime Emmy Awards, six Golden Globe Awards, two Screen Actors Guild Awards, four Grammy Awards, and inducted as a Disney Legend in 2009.
